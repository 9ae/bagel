Trapped in a loveless marriage she can't escape without destroying her immigrant family's hard-won social standing, Elanor, discovers her husband Ken has been sleeping with Diane, a lonely pharmaceutical executive who shares his co-working space. When the two women unexpectedly meet and find themselves drawn together by loneliness, grief, and a darkly mutual wish, what begins as bitter confession over wine slowly curdles into something far more dangerous: a whispered plan to quietly, permanently, solve the problem of Ken.

At its heart, this is a story about two women circling the same wound — one trapped by duty and unspoken identity, the other by romantic delusion — who find each other in the wreckage of the same man, and discover that the most unsettling thing about their meeting isn't the murder plot they're hatching, but how much they recognize themselves in each other.
